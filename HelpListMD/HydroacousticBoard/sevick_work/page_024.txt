When a rod is used as a transformer core, the calculations for the
magnetizing inductance become complicated. This is because of the
effect of the high-reluctance air path external to the core. As will be
explained shortly, the inductance is independent of the rod’s permeabil-
ity. Experimentally, it is on the order of one-half the value of a toroid
with a permeability of 125.
