When matching 50-Q coaxial cable to impedances of 12.5 Q and
lower using toroidal cores, a need arose for transmission lines with
characteristic impedances of 25 Q and lower. These transmission lines
could be used both for the Ruthroff transformer with its “boot-strap”
connection and for the Guanella transformer with its series-parallel
connection. This chapter presents the earlier work of the author on
trifilar windings and striplines, as well as the more recent work on
transformers using coaxial cable windings. Again, actual measurements
of the characteristic impedance, in situ, reveal that the maximum high-
frequency responses are close to that predicted by Ruthroff and
Guanella. The differences are only on the order of 10% for coaxial cable,
and as high as 30% for stripline.
