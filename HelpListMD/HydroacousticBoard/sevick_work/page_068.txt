These 1:4 transformers can be designed basically in two ways: (1)
the Ruthroff method, which uses a single, coiled transmission line and a
feedback (or “boot-strap”) connection in order to sum two voltages, and
(2) the Guanella method, which sums two voltages by using two coiled
transmission lines (and thus equal delays) in a parallel-series connection.
The Guanella transformer, which is basically a balun, requires extra
isolation as an unun and was discussed in Sec 2.3 of Chapter 2.
