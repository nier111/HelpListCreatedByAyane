In the process of winding many practical transformers for the
second edition of this book, I found that tightly wound rod transformers
did agree quite well with Ruthroff’s theory. A characteristic impedance
of 25 Q, using no. 14 wire, did yield the best high-frequency response
at about the 12.5:50-Q level. The question then arises about the results
shown in Fig 2-5. Here a 54-inch diameter rod had the same high-fre-
quency response as a toroidal transformer! In order to understand this
discrepancy, the characteristic impedance was measured on a 5-turn
transmission line of no. 14 wire, tightly wound on a %-inch diameter
rod. The impedance was found to be 26 Q (4 Q higher than expected)
and, connected as a 1:4 unun, the best high-frequency response occurred
at the 16.5:66-Q level. Theoretically, the highest frequency response
should be at the 13:52-Q level. Other experiments with rod transform-
