Using other wire diameters again proved the same finding; namely, the
characteristic impedances were all considerably higher on toroids than
on rods. In fact, some earlier toroidal transformers, with tight windings,
were measured and found to have characteristic impedances as high as
41 Q. Thus there is little doubt that the transformer used in Fig 3-2 had
