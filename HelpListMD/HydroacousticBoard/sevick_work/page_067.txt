by using two transformers in series, and by designing one of the
transformers so that the characteristic impedance of its transmission line
is larger than normally required (that is, Zp > VRLRg ), a canceling of the
reactive components of the two transformers can occur. This results in
a greater bandwidth at the high impedance levels, particularly for
Ruthroff 1:4 transformers. An example is shown in Chapter 9 in the case
of a 12:1 balanced-to-unbalanced transformer designed to match 600 Q
to 50 Q. A transformer following this design will match a 50-Q cable
to arhombic antenna.

This canceling effect can be seen from the plots shown in Figs 1- 5
and 1-6 of Ruthroff’s Eq 1-6 for the input impedance as seen at the
low-impedance end of the transformer.
